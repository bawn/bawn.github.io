Year after year, whenever the end of the year comes around, I am reminded of how quickly time passes. Throughout this year, I kept reminding myself to keep learning and to stay in touch with new things. Now it is time to look back on my 2017.



### WeChat Mini Program

At the beginning of the year, I started developing a WeChat Mini Program version of the company project. Although the design and interactions were simplified, it still counted as my first formal front-end project. Unfortunately, for various reasons, it never made it to launch.

### Trip to Japan

Before setting off, I was full of anticipation for the trip. More than cherry blossoms, what I wanted to understand was Japanese culture and society, because I had once seriously considered living and working in Japan.

A 7-day trip to Japan was busy and fulfilling. Starting from Hangzhou, I visited Osaka Shinsaibashi, Nara Kasuga Taisha, Kyoto Kinkaku-ji, Mount Fuji, Oshino Hakkai in Yamanashi, Ueno Park in Tokyo, Ginza in Tokyo, Kamakura in Kanagawa, Enoshima in Kanagawa, and Peace Park in Kanagawa.

* Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2: the same popcorn-standard sequel as the first one (Douban 8.1)
* Alien: Covenant: Fassbender, please begin your performance (Douban 7.3)
* The Unity of Heroes: Lu Zhengyu clearly put a lot of effort into it, looking forward to his next work (Douban 6.1)
* Wolf Warrior 2: to be honest, I had no intention of watching it (Douban 7.2)
* Gintama: if you are not an anime fan, skip it (Douban 7.1)
* Kingsman: The Golden Circle: did Matthew Vaughn secretly rewrite the script? (Douban 7.0)
* Blade Runner 2049: nearly three hours of artistic enjoyment (Douban 8.5)
* The Bad Genius: do we really have to be politically correct? (Douban 8.2)

* Justice League: DC had a strong hand but wasted it (Douban 6.6)
* Coco: family, dreams, and growth (Douban 9.1)
* Legend of the Demon Cat: maybe the novel is even better (Douban 6.9)

### Swift

At the end of May, Swift was finally introduced into the project. Before that, I had been cautious about Swift, mainly because of the instability of its syntax. Overall, bringing Swift into the project was more beneficial than harmful.

### React Native

This year I continued learning React Native and open-sourced the [noder-react-native](https://github.com/bawn/noder-react-native) project, a [cnodejs](https://cnodejs.org/) forum client built with React Native. I handled the entire project myself, from design to product to development, and Sketch helped a lot on the design side.

### Ukulele

I am glad that near the end of the year I managed to rediscover my interest in learning the ukulele. Relearning "Castle in the Sky" was much easier than I expected, and I may soon be able to play the accompaniment to "Loveing" fluently. The Dm chord is no longer hard to press either.

### Tutoring

Throughout the year, I intermittently guided my cousin in learning iOS development. Unlike a blog, which expresses ideas in writing, face-to-face guidance requires explaining knowledge in a clear and easy-to-understand way. Clearly, I am not very good at that, but in the end my clumsy guidance still helped him improve quite a bit.

### A Fresh Start

There are still many unknown things waiting for me to explore in 2018. Hello, 2018.
